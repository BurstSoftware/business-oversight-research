In Minnesota, what state department oversees business practices and policies for employers 

In Minnesota, employment-related business practices and policies for employers — such as wage and hour laws, workplace safety, labor standards, and employer compliance with workplace-related state laws — are primarily overseen by the:

🏛️ Minnesota Department of Labor and Industry (DLI)
This state agency handles most workplace and employment-law regulation for employers and employees in Minnesota. It administers and enforces state labor standards including minimum wage, overtime, wage payment, child labor rules, workplace safety and health (including MNOSHA), workers’ compensation, and employment practices compliance.
The DLI’s Labor Standards Division specifically deals with enforcement of many employment laws and helps employers comply with state labor requirements.
📌 Other Agencies With Roles in Employer Regulations

In addition to DLI, several other state departments/officials play secondary or specialized roles in employer/business policies:

Minnesota Department of Employment and Economic Development (DEED) — supports workforce development and provides employer guidance and resources (e.g., employer guides, labor market info).
Minnesota Department of Commerce — regulates certain business practices and participates in enforcement partnerships (like worker misclassification enforcement) along with other agencies.
Minnesota Attorney General’s Office — enforces certain wage-related laws and can investigate labor violations like wage theft or misclassification in some cases.
Minnesota Secretary of State — registers business entities (corporations, LLCs, etc.), though it doesn’t oversee labor/employment policy.
Summary
Primary regulator of employment practices and employer compliance in Minnesota: Minnesota Department of Labor and Industry (DLI).
Supporting roles: DEED, Department of Commerce, Attorney General’s Office, Secretary of State.
